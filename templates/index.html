What is Water Test?

Safe and available water is essential for public health, whether it is used for drinking, household purposes, food production or recreational purposes.

Water quality index (WQI/Water Quality Index) is an important measuring tool for determining water quality as an index that reflects the combined influence of various water quality parameters that are considered and taken for calculating the water quality index.

So, we took some inspiration to use dataset water quality index (WQI) to understand the types of safe drinking water by applying machine learning to distinguish between drinking water and water that is not suitable for drinking.

Water test is an application based website to predict the level of water quality which can provide prediction results whether the water is safe for consumption or not suitable for consumption and suggest the probable disease that can be caused by these water type.

What's that Water Quality Index?

"Save Water, Save Life"

Water quality index (WQI) is a simple method used as part of water quality surveys that are influenced by natural processes and human activities. The Water Quality Index was developed with the concept that a higher index value indicates better water quality. The proposed method for comparing water quality from various water sources is based on eight water quality parameters such as Fecal, pH, Temperaure, Phosphorus, Oxygen, total sediment, Nitrogen and Turbidity.

The Importance of Water Quality Monitoring

  1. As a Basic Human Need

    Water is one of the most basic needs to support human life. This reflects how important water is in human life. Parameters for measuring water quality, whether physical, chemical or bacteriological, must comply with the standards.

  2. Quality that is safe for consumption

    Water quality that is safe for consumption is water quality that meets good physical criteria (temperature, color, sedimentation); Chemical (Oxygen, pH, Salinity, Nutrients); and Biological (Bacteria, Algae, Phytoplankton).
